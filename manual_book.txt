MOONCREST MANUAL BOOK FOR GUIDE DEPARTMENT

# Rules
All guides must adhere to the following rules at all times to maintain order, safety, and
respect during expeditions.
- Confidentiality: It is prohibited to leak or share this manual or internal team
information with anyone outside the expedition team.
- Professional Conduct: Always act with respect towards team members and
participants. Your behavior reflects the entire group.
- Safety First: Prioritize your own safety and the safety of others at all times. Never take unnecessary risks.
- Follow Instructions: Listen to your commander or higher-ranking staff. Cooperation is key to success.
- No Abuse of Authority: As a guide, you are here to help, not to dominate. Treat participants fairly and equally.
- Always Be Prepared: Always arrive with the proper equipment, mindset, and knowledge for the expedition.
- Report Issues: If you see a problem, hazard, or rule violation, report it to the leadership immediately.

# Guide Professionalism
- Being professional and mature is important.
- Avoid breaking any rules.
- Be flexible with participants, do not make offensive jokes, and have patience.
- Be friendly; being professional does not mean being serious, but being responsible.
- Always ensure you have enough time to host without having to leave halfway through.
- Always check if your group is following. If you do not have a co-host, stop every few minutes to ensure everyone can catch up.
- Taking note of your participants' names is good practice for tracking the group

# Punishment for offenders
- First Strike = Warning
- Second Strike = Muted
- Third Strike = Removal from the department guide position and will be kicked out.

# Guide Rank
- Probation Guide (Pass the test with the Instructor or Director)
- Mooncrest Junior Guide (10 times host or good performance)
- Mooncrest Senior Guide (25 times host or good performance)
- Mooncrest Director of Guide (Handpicked)

# Types of Expeditions
- Normal Expedition: A standard expedition held on official mountains and routes.
- Joint Expedition: An expedition conducted in collaboration with one or more allied expedition groups.
- Expedition Event: There are usually prizes, only conducted by Senior Guides and above, and permitted by the owner.

# Expedition Protocols

## If the Host Dies/Is Impeded During the Climb
- More than 5 minutes from the summit: Ask the Co-Host  to lead the group to a safe place and wait for you, or continue leading if waiting takes too long.
- If there is no Co-Host, choose one participant to lead.
- Less than 5 minutes from the summit: If you cannot catch up, ask the Co-Host or atrusted participant to take the summit photo for you.

## If the Host Must Leave in the Middle of an Expedition
- You are responsible for appointing a leader before leaving.
- Transfer leadership to the Co-Host
- If none are available, appoint a trusted participant to lead and ask them to provide a screenshot at the summit.
- A Co-Host who takes over officially becomes the new Host for the remainder of the expedition.

## If the Co-Host Leaves
- If the Co-Host leaves after completing at least half of the route, they will still be counted as a Co-Host.
- If they leave before the halfway point, they are not counted as a Co-Host.

## Canceling an Expedition
- If no one reacts to the Expedition Shout
- If no climbers show up

## Role of Co-Host 
- Maximum of 2 Co-Hosts per expedition.
- Co-Hosts must assist the Host, ensure everyone is following, wait for AFK climbers, and guide participants who are lagging.

# Managing Climbers

## Warning Climbers
Guides are authorized to enforce rules and issue warnings. Warnings should be given for the following situations:
- Too far ahead of the guide: Being out of sight or more than 5 minutes ahead.
-Disruptive or Disrespectful Behavior: Persistently pushing climbers on ladders, blocking paths, etc.
-Light jokes are fine, but not to the point of being disrespectful.
- Violating Roblox ToS: Including hacking, glitching, swearing, discrimination, or verbally attacking others.
- Ignoring Instructions: Repeatedly ignoring instructions from the Host or Co-Host.

## Procedure
1. As a general rule, politely ask the climber to stop first before issuing a formal warning.
2. In severe cases, you may directly issue a warning or remove the participant (if you have valid proof).
3. Typically, a participant who receives three warnings will be removed from the expedition.
4. All warnings given must be reported to the Guide Director.
